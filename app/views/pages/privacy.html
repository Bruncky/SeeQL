What data do we collect?

SeeQL collects the following data:

  • Personal identification information (full name and e-mail address)
  • Any data that is (strictly) necessary to connect one or more databases to your projects
  • Any data that is normally sent as part of a basic HTTP request

How do we collect your data?

You directly provide SeeQL with most of the data we collect. We collect and process data when you sign up for an account, as well as when you connect one or more databases to your projects.

How will we use your data?

SeeQL collects your data so that we can:

  • Assign created projects to you and manage your account.
  • Connect one or more databases to your projects
  • Keep you updated about account information changes (name, e-mail, avatar).
  • Keep you updated about any changes to this privacy policy.

Our service does not send your data to any third party for marketing and/or analytical purposes. We are strictly against such practices, and make a point of collecting as little data as possible about you, while being transparent about what we do collect.

When you create an account, a project, or connect a database to your project, all relevant data is processed directly within the (Ruby on Rails) app, which is hosted on a Heroku dyno.

How do we connect a database to your project? What information do we access?

When you connect a database to your project, you have to provide all the necessary credentials, just as you would when using an app such as DBeaver or Postico. When you submit the form, we open a connection to the provided database using those credentials. We then read out the structure of your database by accessing an internal schema called information_schema.tables. This schema holds general information about the database, such as table names, column names, and a list of foreign key constraints. After reading out this information, we close the connection right away.

With that information in hand, we then create a Database object in our own app's database. This object is connected to several Table objects, which in turn is connected to several Column objects. All of this is then used to generate the table structure that you see in the visualiser.

How do we store your data?

SeeQL is hosted on a Heroku dyno, and securely stores your data on a PostgreSQL database hosted on AWS (Amazon Web Services). Heroku is a PaaS (Platform as a Service) owned by Salesforce.com, Inc. You can read their privacy policies here:

  • Amazon AWS: https://aws.amazon.com/privacy/
  • Heroku/Salesforce: https://www.salesforce.com/company/privacy/

SeeQL will keep all of your data for as long as you have an account on our website. If you decide do delete your account (which can be done from the 'Edit Profile' page), your data will be flagged for deletion and kept for a grace period of 30 days, during which you may reactivate your account. After 30 days, all of your data will be completely and irreversably removed from our app.

What are your data protection rights?

SeeQL would like to make sure you are fully aware of all of your data protection rights. Every user is entitled to the following:

The right to access – You have the right to request SeeQL for copies of your personal data. We may charge you a small fee for this service.

The right to rectification – You have the right to request that SeeQL correct any information you believe is inaccurate. You also have the right to request SeeQL to complete the information you believe is incomplete.

The right to erasure – You have the right to request that SeeQL erase your personal data, under certain conditions.

The right to restrict processing – You have the right to request that SeeQL restrict the processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.

The right to object to processing – You have the right to object to Our Company’s processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.

The right to data portability – You have the right to request that SeeQL transfer the data that we have collected to another organization, or directly to you, under certain conditions.
